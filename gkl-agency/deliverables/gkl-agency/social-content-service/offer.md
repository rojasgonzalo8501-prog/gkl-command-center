# GKL — "Done-for-you content calendar" offer

> The machine you built for clients is a product you can sell. Every business needs content;
> almost none want to make it. You already run the calendar — now package it as a monthly retainer.
>
> **The pitch is the outcome, not the AI.** Lead with the pain they already feel. How you deliver
> (one agent, four roles, Blotato auto-publishing) is your margin — not the sales line.

## Who buys — and the pain to lead with

| Segment | Who | The pain they already feel | The hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Local & service businesses** | Clinics, gyms, restaurants, real estate, salons | "We know we *should* be posting, but nobody ever does it." | "I run your whole content calendar — you never touch it." |
| **Founders & solopreneurs** | Early product/SaaS, e-com, agency owners | "Building the product is the job. Marketing slips every single week." | "You ship the product. I keep you visible — hands-off." |
| **Personal brands & coaches** | Coaches, consultants, creators | "My face *is* the business, and I can't repurpose my own content fast enough." | "Give me your raw content once — I turn it into a month across every channel." |

**Rule:** open with the pain, not "AI-powered". The AI is *how* you deliver a team's output
solo — it is never the reason they buy.

## The packages (monthly retainer — never hourly)

Sell the outcome per month. Hourly caps your upside and invites nickel-and-diming; a retainer
sells a result and compounds. Prices in SEK, tuned for the Swedish SMB market — adjust per prospect.

> **Why three tiers:** Starter removes the risk objection and gets a yes. Growth is where most
> land and where auto-publishing earns its margin. Done-for-you anchors the price so Growth looks
> like the sensible middle. Always quote all three; let them self-select up.

## Positioning & margin logic
- **Cost to deliver is near-flat across tiers** (same 4-role agent), so each step up is mostly margin.
- **Auto-publishing is the moat:** competitors hand over a spreadsheet; you hand over *posted*.
- **Compliance-heavy niches** (health, finance, legal) command a premium — you already handle the
  hard gate (see Vivanord). Price those 20–40% above the table.

## Handling the three objections you'll actually hear
1. **"Is this just AI content?"** → "It's my process — a strategist, copywriter, editor and
   designer's worth of output, run by me. You get a named human who owns the result." Show the
   Vivanord batch as proof of quality + compliance.
2. **"We tried an agency and it was generic."** → Starter, one month, one channel. "If the first
   batch doesn't sound like you, you walk. Most don't." Low risk, land-and-expand.
3. **"We can do it ourselves."** → "You can. You haven't for six months. The retainer is the
   forcing function — it goes out whether or not you have a good week."

## Onboarding a new client (repeatable)
1. Discovery: brand voice, channels, 3 accounts they admire, any compliance rules.
2. Spin up `clients/[client]/` (CLAUDE.md + brief) — same structure as existing clients.
3. First batch via `workflows/social_media_content.md` (4 roles).
4. Connect their socials in Blotato (`workflows/blotato_setup.md`), get manifest approved.
5. Publish, report, expand Starter → Growth → Done-for-you.
