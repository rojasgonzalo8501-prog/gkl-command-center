## 1. Website & UX Audit

### Critical Issues

**URL Structure is broken for SEO**
Current: `/17/2/bildemontering/` `/17/3/bildelar-verkstad/`
This is a legacy CMS artifact. Google reads "17", "2", "3" as meaningless numbers.
Fix: `/bildemontering/` `/bildelar/` `/verkstad/` `/kontakt/`

**Email is telia.com**
`mercaskroten@telia.com` signals a business frozen in 2005. Every email sent from this address erodes trust.
Fix: `adam@merca-skroten.se` or `info@merca-skroten.se`

**No opening hours anywhere**
Not on the website, not on the contact page. A customer can't know when to call.

**No response time promise**
The contact form submits with "your email has been sent" — no indication of when they'll hear back.

**"Simpler workshop services" — major undersell**
They describe their own workshop as offering only "simpler" tasks. This is self-defeating copy.
Fix: "Full workshop services including windshield replacement, tire mounting, and parts installation."

**No WhatsApp**
WhatsApp Business is standard for Swedish SMEs. Customers often prefer it over forms and email.

### Missing Pages
- FAQ (What happens to my car? How long does pickup take? Do I need the registration?)
- About Us / Adam's story (40 years, new owner 2020 — great narrative)
- Specific Mercedes parts catalog or even a basic inventory list
- Delivery/shipping info for parts

## 5. Conversion Audit

### Current Conversion Rate: Very Low

**No pricing transparency**
Customers have to call or email to get any pricing. In 2026, this means they bounce to a competitor who gives them an instant quote.

**No instant quote tool**
A simple form: "Enter your car's registration → Get a price in 60 seconds" is table stakes in the Swedish bilskrot market. Competitors offer this.

**No urgency or scarcity signals**
No "limited pickup slots", no "book before Friday" — nothing that drives action now vs. later.

**Contact form friction**
- No inline validation
- No response time promise after submission
- No WhatsApp alternative
- No phone hours listed
