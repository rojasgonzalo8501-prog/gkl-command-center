Part 5 — The Clothing Brand

Yalo Clothing deserves to be a brand, not a footnote.

The activewear line is already born. It just needs positioning, pricing confidence, and a launch strategy worthy of the brand.

Fix This Now
End the permanent sale
Run one proper 72-hour sale to clear current stock if needed, then restore full pricing permanently. Use scarcity (limited colorways, seasonal drops) instead of ongoing discounts. Lululemon leggings at $128 sell better than Yalo leggings at $26 — not because of quality, but because of perceived value. Price communicates quality.
The Strategy
Seasonal drops, not a permanent catalogue
Move from "always available, always discounted" to 2–3 seasonal drops per year. Limited colorways, defined launch windows, waitlists. This is how Supreme built hype and how Lululemon keeps full-price integrity. Each drop becomes an event that drives content, email opens, and social media excitement.
The Identity
Mediterranean movement — not generic activewear
The clothing needs a visual identity tied to Artemis's Greek roots. Ocean blues, sandy neutrals, earthy terracottas — the colors are already there in the product names (Balos Sand, Ocean Blue, Earth Clay). That's the visual language. Make it deliberate. Make it the brand story. Shoot it in Greece if possible.
The Growth Engine
Creator collabs → audience crossover
The Aleksandra collabs are already proving this works. Expand it. Partner with 5–8 yoga teachers and wellness creators who each have 10K–100K engaged followers. Send them the collection, co-create content, give them a discount code. Each partnership is a content machine and a new audience acquisition. Cost: product samples. ROI: potentially thousands of new customers.
Part 6 — Content Creation & Digital Marketing

Content is the engine.
Everything else runs on it.

Products, courses, and apps don't sell themselves — content is what drives discovery, trust, and conversion. Artemis has a story, a face, a mission, and a visual world that is genuinely magnetic. Here is how we turn that into a content machine that works 24/7.

TikTok — The Growth Engine
Artemis goes viral. The algorithm is ready.
Yoga is one of TikTok's highest-performing niches. A Greek founder, former figure skater, mother of two, living in Sweden, making cork mats to fight Mediterranean plastic pollution — that is a story the algorithm rewards with millions of views. We create a TikTok content system: weekly posting schedule, scripted hooks for Artemis, POV formats, day-in-the-life yoga routines, cork mat education, and sustainability storytelling. One viral video can bring 50,000 new eyes to the brand overnight.
YouTube — The Long Game
Free classes that compound forever.
Yoga with Adriene built 12 million subscribers and a multi-million dollar business on free YouTube content. Every class she uploads is a permanent SEO asset — found by people searching "yoga for beginners", "30-minute yoga flow", "yoga for anxiety". We launch Yalo's YouTube channel with 2 classes per month. Each video links to the app, the courses, and the shop. The content pays dividends for years after posting.
Instagram Reels — Transformation
Stop posting for followers. Post for discovery.
880 posts, 2,373 followers means static content isn't working. We completely shift the Instagram strategy: 80% Reels, 20% Stories. Reels formats that work for yoga brands: 60-second practice clips, "cork mat vs. regular mat" comparisons, Greek coastline transitions, behind-the-scenes mat production, morning routine content. Every Reel is designed to reach non-followers first. We also audit and clean up the feed so that first-time visitors instantly understand what Yalo Yoga is and why they want it.
Email Marketing
The audience you own.
Unlike Instagram or TikTok, your email list belongs to you — no algorithm can take it away. We build a full email marketing system: a 5-email welcome sequence that tells Artemis's story and introduces the products and courses; a weekly newsletter (The Yalo Letter) with short yoga tips, sustainability facts, and product highlights; automated post-purchase sequences; and launch campaigns for each new course and seasonal clothing drop. Email converts 3–5× better than social media for product sales.
SEO Content Strategy
Rank for searches that have buying intent.
The blog exists but isn't working hard enough. We build a keyword-led content cluster strategy: pillar pages for "cork yoga mat", "sustainable yoga mat", "yoga for beginners Sweden" plus supporting articles for each. Every product page gets fully optimized descriptions with target keywords. The goal is organic traffic from people who are already searching for exactly what Yalo sells. 12–18 months of consistent SEO content can generate thousands of visitors per month at zero ongoing cost.
Brand Photography & Visual Identity
One Greece shoot. Months of content.
The most powerful content Yalo can produce is Artemis practicing yoga on a Greek coastline — cork mat on golden sand, olive groves in the background, clear Aegean water. Mediterranean aesthetics are having a cultural moment and this brand is uniquely positioned to own it. We coordinate one focused content shoot in Greece (or a Mediterranean-aesthetic location in Sweden as an alternative): 3–4 hours, clothing + mats, multiple locations. Output: 200+ photos and 15–20 short video clips that feed all channels for 3–6 months.
Pinterest — The Evergreen Discovery Channel
The platform that sends buyers, not just viewers.
Pinterest users are in "planning" mode — looking for yoga inspiration, sustainable lifestyle ideas, activewear, home gym aesthetics. Yalo's visual world (Mediterranean colors, cork textures, nature) is exactly what performs on Pinterest. Pins have a lifespan measured in months, not hours. We create a Pinterest presence with optimized boards (sustainable yoga, cork yoga mats, Mediterranean lifestyle, yoga for mothers), regular pinning schedule, and direct product links. Low effort, long tail traffic that compounds over time.
Paid Advertising — When Organic Needs a Boost
Targeted reach. Measurable ROI.
Once the organic content engine is running, paid advertising amplifies what's working. Meta ads targeting yoga practitioners, sustainable living enthusiasts, and wellness consumers in Europe and North America. Retargeting campaigns for people who visited the store but didn't buy. Course launch ads driving traffic to the free 7-day challenge lead magnet. We don't run ads until the funnel is ready to convert — paid traffic to a broken funnel is money wasted. Done correctly, €500/month in ads can generate €2,000–5,000 in revenue.
