## 1. Product Audit

### Critical: Hero Product Is Sold Out
The Golden Hour Yoga Mat is their best-reviewed (5★), highest-profile product (a collaboration with Aleksandra/Golden Hour Yoga). It's **sold out** with no back-in-stock notification. Every visitor who wants their hero product leaves empty-handed. This is a revenue bleed.

### Critical: Permanent Discounting Destroys Brand Value
The entire activewear line is running at ~50% off permanently:
- Flow Leggings: $26 (was $50)
- Wave Bra: $24 (was $50)
- Lumen Leggings: $33 (was $50)

When everything is always on sale, the "sale price" becomes the real price. Customers lose trust in the original pricing. Premium yoga brands (Lululemon, Alo Yoga) never do this — they protect their pricing and use scarcity instead.

### Small Catalog Risk
8 products total (3 cork + 5 activewear). One sold-out hero means the effective catalog is 7 items. That's not enough for SEO, not enough for upsells, not enough for a returning customer to find something new.

### Collaboration Strategy is Underexplored
The "Aurea By Aleksandra X YaloYoga" collab and the Golden Hour Mat collab both show Artemis can work with creators. This is a growth lever — more creator collabs = more audience crossover.

## 2. Social Media Audit

### Instagram: 880 posts, 2,373 followers — The Math Doesn't Work
If you've posted 880 times and have 2,373 followers, you're averaging less than 3 new followers per post. That means the content isn't attracting new audiences — it's only reaching people already following. This points to:
- Content not optimized for discovery (wrong hashtags, no Reels strategy)
- No TikTok feeding followers to Instagram
- No viral moments or viral-worthy content format
- Bio "DREAM - CREATE - INSPIRE" tells no one what Yalo Yoga does or sells

### No TikTok — In 2026, That's a Massive Blind Spot
Yoga content is among the top performing categories on TikTok. Creators with Artemis's story — Greek founder, figure skater turned yogi, sustainability mission, mother — go viral regularly on TikTok. She has everything TikTok rewards and she's not there.

### No YouTube
Long-form yoga content on YouTube drives SEO, trust, and recurring traffic. Channels like Yoga with Adriene have millions of followers built entirely on free YouTube content monetized through product sales and courses. This is exactly Yalo's model opportunity.

### Content Themes Need a Reset
Current blog: 9 posts in 5 years, irregular, mostly product education. Missing:
- Artemis on camera (founder content converts)
- Yoga practices (the reason people follow yoga brands)
- Behind-the-scenes sustainability content
- Community content (student transformations, challenges)
- Greece/ocean/nature content (powerful visual identity)

## 4. Revenue Model Audit

### Current: One Stream (Physical Products)
All revenue comes from selling physical products. No recurring revenue, no digital products, no community revenue.

### The Three Missing Revenue Streams

**Stream 1: Yoga App** (Biggest opportunity)
Market: $20B online yoga market growing at 18% CAGR
Model: Freemium app with $9.99–14.99/month subscription
What's inside: Artemis's yoga classes, breathing practices, community
Integration: "Buy a mat, get 3 months free" — product and app reinforce each other
Revenue potential at 1,000 subscribers: €120,000–180,000/year (recurring)

**Stream 2: Online Courses**
Model: Self-paced courses at €97–297
Options: "Foundations of Sustainable Yoga" (beginner), "Flow & Breathe" (intermediate), "Yoga for Busy Mothers" (niche, taps Artemis's own journey)
Revenue potential: 100 sales/month at €97 = €9,700/month

**Stream 3: Clothing Brand (needs positioning fix)**
Already launched but pricing strategy is broken. The activewear needs:
- Clear identity distinct from the cork products
- Proper seasonal launches with scarcity (not permanent sale)
- Sustainability certifications for the fabric
- Size range expansion
- Influencer seeding

## 6. Growth Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Fix the Foundation (Month 1)
- Restock or replace the Golden Hour Mat (or at minimum add back-in-stock signup)
- End permanent discounting — run a proper 72-hour sale, then restore full pricing
- Fix Instagram bio: clear value proposition, link to hero product
- Start TikTok — Artemis introduces herself and the brand story (3-5 videos)
- Fix SEO on cork collection pages with keyword-optimized descriptions
- Add product bundles: Mat + Blocks + Bag at a real bundle discount

### Phase 2 — Digital Products (Month 1–3)
- Launch first online course: "Foundations of Cork & Flow" — 4 weeks, beginner-friendly
- Build email welcome sequence that sells the course passively
- Plan app MVP: class library, daily practice, community tab
- Launch waitlist for the app with a "founding member" price lock

### Phase 3 — Social Media Transformation (Month 2–4)
- TikTok: Artemis on camera — her story, her routines, her cork mat
- YouTube: 2 yoga classes per month (SEO goldmine)
- Instagram Reels: short practices, sustainability facts, behind-scenes Greece content
- Collaborate with 3–5 yoga micro-influencers (10K–100K followers)
- Join and engage with sustainable living and yoga communities

### Phase 4 — App Launch & Community (Month 4–8)
- App launch (MVP via white-label platform like Uscreen or custom)
- Founding member subscription: €7.99/month for first 500 users
- "Yalo Community" — monthly membership with live classes, challenges, Q&A with Artemis
- Ambassador program: send free mat + clothing to 20 yoga teachers, get content in return
